Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Measure before you buy. Note your cabinet width, depth, and the height of the P-trap pipe. The best over-toilet under-sink organizers fit your exact space, so a few minutes with a tape measure saves a return.
  • Decide between bins and drawers. Stackable bins use vertical height and lift out for cleaning. Pull-out drawers and trays bring the back of the cabinet to you without crouching.
  • Clear plastic shows you what you own. See-through walls cut down on duplicate buys, while coated-metal racks hold more weight for hair dryers and towel stacks.
  • Mind the pipes. Two-tier racks like the Delamu and DEKAVA leave the back open so the P-trap passes through; rigid one-piece bins do not.
  • Price tracks size, not quality here. Our picks run from $15.98 to $37.99, and the cheapest option still earns a 4-star rating.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between an over-toilet and an under-sink organizer?

An over-toilet organizer is a tall freestanding rack or cabinet that uses the empty wall space above the tank, while an under-sink organizer is a bin, drawer, or shelf that fits inside the cabinet beneath your basin and works around the P-trap pipes. Most bathrooms benefit from both: the over-toilet unit holds towels and decor you reach often, and the under-sink pieces corral cleaning sprays and backups out of sight. The best over-toilet under-sink organizers cover both zones so the whole room stays tidy.

How do I fit an under-sink organizer around the pipes?

Measure the cabinet width, depth, and the height of your P-trap before you buy. Two-tier sliding drawers like the Delamu and DEKAVA leave the back open so the pipe passes through, and pull-out trays such as the Ukeetap slide forward without hitting the trap. For deep cabinets, stackable clear bins like our Vtopmart pick let you build storage on either side of the pipe and lift each box out as a unit.

Are clear plastic or metal organizers better for a bathroom?

Clear plastic bins win on visibility and price, so you can spot a refill at a glance and wipe them clean in the sink. Metal wire racks hold more weight and shrug off humidity, which suits heavier items like hair dryers or stacks of folded towels. If you store small toiletries, go clear plastic; if you store bulky or heavy gear, choose a coated-metal frame.

Which over-toilet under-sink organizer should I buy first?

Start with the Vtopmart 3 Pack Clear Stackable at $35.99 if your cabinet is deep and you want to see everything you own. If the pipe blocks a one-piece bin, the Delamu 2-Tier metal rack at $25.99 adapts around it. On a tight budget, the Ukeetap pull-out tray at $15.98 adds reach to a shallow cabinet for the least money.
